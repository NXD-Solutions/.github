# Compression signals understanding

## 2026-04-12 — New principle under Pattern-seeking by Instinct

**Trigger:** Recurring observation during self-maintain scan — "problems better understood, collapses the implementation" — examined through Socratic inquiry. IFD analysis revealed: (1) the observation is more fundamental than IFD; (2) IFD is one implementation path, not the only one; (3) no existing principle names compression as the quality signal of understanding regardless of mechanism.

**Decision:** Add a new principle "Compression signals understanding" as a sibling to "Find the pattern, not the instance" under Pattern-seeking by Instinct. The principle names compression as a quality signal of deepened understanding, mechanism-agnostic — pattern-seeking, experience, deliberation, failed implementation, and evidence arrival all qualify as paths.

**Ruled out:**
- *Lifecycle dimension (placement, deferral, promotion):* Challenged during deliberation — compression is epistemological; lifecycle is operational. Bundling produces a principle doing two things. The lifecycle dimension is already covered: "Start lean, don't close the door" handles honest placement; "What holds for one holds for many" and "Build once for many" handle promotion signals. Stripped.
- *Universal claim (understanding always compresses):* Challenged — understanding can reveal the problem is larger than assumed (honest scope expansion). Compression framed as quality signal, not guaranteed outcome.
- *Expanding "Find the pattern":* The gap is not that "Find the pattern" is incomplete — it correctly names pattern-seeking → compression. The gap is that compression is attributed to one mechanism when it belongs to a broader driver. A new sibling is cleaner than overloading the existing principle.

**Tests:** S1–S4 pass. P1/P3 with "Find the pattern" pass — independent and additive (compression from experience satisfies this principle without satisfying the sibling). Distillation: no words to cut.

**Conviction (S|L): high | high**
