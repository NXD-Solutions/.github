# Evidence serves the reader, not the author

## Trigger

Recurring pattern: a claim lands without evidence. The reader's next action is always "can I see the evidence?" — a question that should never need asking because the answer should already be present.

## Decision

Add a rule with two thresholds — comprehension and challenge — that size evidence at point of claim. Placed in `communication.md` alongside reader-need filter.

## Why two thresholds, not one

A single threshold ("enough") is subjective. Two thresholds name the reader's two distinct needs: understanding the claim (comprehension) and being able to dispute it (challenge). Below comprehension the claim is opaque; between comprehension and challenge it is persuasive but unchallengeable; above challenge it is noise.

## Relationship to reader-need filter

Reader-need filter cuts content the reader doesn't need. This rule ensures content the reader *does* need is present. They are complementary — one prevents excess, the other prevents gaps.

## Meta-observation

This rule is an instance of a broader pattern: predict the reader's next question and answer it before they ask. That meta-pattern was noted but not codified — it risks being too broad (could justify over-engineering). The evidence rule captures the highest-value instance. The meta-pattern is tracked separately.
