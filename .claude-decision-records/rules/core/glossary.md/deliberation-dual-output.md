# Decision Record — Deliberation (dual output revision)

**Item:** `glossary.md / Deliberation`
**Date:** 2026-03-30
**Conviction:** S: high | L: high

---

## Trigger

The original Deliberation definition said it "produces a Decision Record on conviction." With the CR now carrying a mandatory Deliberation section, the DR is no longer the only output — the Deliberation section in the CR records the outcome too. The definition was incomplete.

Simultaneously, the original definition scoped deliberation to "a Change Request." This was challenged: deliberation predates CRs, runs in SM sessions unrelated to CRs, and applies to proposals that never become formal CRs. Constraining to "Change Request" would make the CR the owner of deliberation — inverting the relationship. Deliberation is the parent process; CR is one formal trigger.

## Decision

Two changes:
1. Replace "a Change Request" with "a proposal" — deliberation is not owned by the CR.
2. Replace "produces a Decision Record on conviction" with dual output: a Deliberation section in the CR recording the outcome, and a Decision Record when the reasoning is non-obvious or consequential.

## Why "a proposal" not "a Change Request"

Deliberation is a process that applies to any proposal under examination — formal or informal. The CR is the formal artefact that triggers deliberation in the governed path, but the process itself is broader. Constraining the definition would embed a hierarchy error: the CR would appear to own deliberation, when deliberation governs the CR.

The enforcement gap for informal proposals (no Deliberation section required) is real but pre-existing — not created by this definition. The Deliberation section rule covers the formal CR path.

## Why dual output

The Deliberation section records whether conviction was reached or the proposal was abandoned. This is the gate — it confirms the process completed. The Decision Record captures the full reasoning when it is non-obvious or consequential — this is the record, serving future editors. Both outputs exist; neither replaces the other. The original definition named only the DR, making the Deliberation section invisible as an output.
