## Primitive — recognising, not finding; perspective-relative

**Date:** 2026-04-09
**Authors:** Peter Sixhøj & Claude Sonnet 4.6
**Concern:** The Primitive entry contained two issues: "Finding the right primitive" named the wrong cognitive act (search, not perception), and "the right primitive" implied a view-independent optimum that doesn't exist — primitives are perspective-relative.
**Decision:** Replace "Finding the right primitive" with "Recognising a primitive" — the cognitive act that succeeds is perception, not search. Drop "the right" — there is no single optimum, only a primitive that compresses from the current vantage point. Add `See: Perspective` to make perspective-relativity visible to a reader encountering the term in isolation. Drop "Today's design is tomorrow's starting point" — a DNA disposition, not a property of what a primitive is; its natural home is already in *Evolving by Instinct*.

**Alternatives considered:** Adding explicit prose about perspective-dependency — rejected because `See: Perspective` closes the gap without adding prose, and the Perspective entry already states the claim. Retaining "the right primitive" with a qualifier — rejected because the article change ("a primitive") is the structural correction; a qualifier would add prose to fix what word choice should fix.
**Trigger:** IFD restructuring session — both issues resolved in conversation but never committed
**Conviction:** high | high
**Value:** correctness (definition names the right cognitive act and makes no stronger claim than it can support), waste eliminated (disposition sentence removed from definition), context preservation (perspective-dependency made visible via See:)
**Notes:** First instance of `See:` used for conceptual dependency rather than strict definitional dependency. The definition text does not use the word Perspective, but correct understanding of Primitive requires it. The convention covers this — the intent of See: is to make the network visible.
