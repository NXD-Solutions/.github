# The DNA governs by what is true, not by what is stated

**File:** `rules/core/constitution.md`
**Category:** Constitutional clause
**Date:** 2026-03-28

---

## Trigger

Adding the principle *Every communicative artefact serves the reader's context* exposed a universal problem: principles with preconditions fire even when those preconditions are already satisfied by context. A boundary clause on each principle would solve the instance but not the pattern. The pattern required a governing statement above all principles.

## What was decided

Create `rules/core/constitution.md` as a new structural category — **Constitution** — above the three domains (Culture, Architecture, Engineering) in the rule network. The file contains two sentences:

> The DNA governs by what is true, not by what is stated. Rules bind when their conditions hold; rules are satisfied when their conditions are already met.

## Why a new file, not a section in principles.md

The Constitution governs how principles operate. Principles derive from strands. Mixing them in the same file conflates two categorically distinct things. A file named "Principles" should not contain the document that governs principles.

Its own file gives it a clean distribution path and makes its nature immediately visible.

## Why Constitution, not a strand or principle

It is not a value NXD is moving toward — strands are. It is not derived from a strand — principles are. It polices how the entire rule network operates, cross-cutting all domains. No existing structural home fit. The structure was extended.

## What the two sentences do

**Sentence 1 — authority:** The DNA governs by what is true, not by what is stated. Authority derives from conditions holding, not from rules being cited. A rule applies even if not invoked; a rule is satisfied even without explicit performance.

**Sentence 2 — operational consequence:** Three cases follow:
- Conditions missing → blocked
- Conditions present → satisfied, no gate fires
- Conditions partially present → only the gap blocks

This eliminates unnecessary overhead. The prompt-response case: audience, topic, and trigger are given by the question itself — the *Define Audience* rule is already satisfied. Without the Constitution, every principle feels like overhead. With it, only unmet conditions require action.

## Resolution: "any observer" vs. "specific reader" (inherited from serve-the-readers-context.md)

Carried by the Constitution: "rules are satisfied when their conditions are already met" makes explicit that "any observer" (don't assume system knowledge) and "specific reader" (organise for who will actually read this) are complementary, not contradictory. The strand sets the floor; the principle sets the organising input. Both conditions can be satisfied simultaneously.

Conviction (S|L): high | high — both parties, 2026-03-28.
