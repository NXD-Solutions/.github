## Trigger

During Author mode, a new self-improvement note described the standing meta-definitions in the workbench as pre-satisfying the conditions of the communicative artefact principle. The observation was: "what is true of one is true for many" — the same audience, topic, and trigger hold for every explanation page. This surfaced as a candidate principle: universality is the prior, not a conclusion.

## What was decided

Add the principle *What holds for one holds for many — uniqueness requires justification* under Pattern-seeking by Instinct. One sentence — the contrast half ("uniqueness requires justification") is not redundant with "until shown otherwise"; it names the active demand the prior places on exceptions.

## Why

Two existing principles under Pattern-seeking by Instinct — *Find the pattern, not the instance* and *Build once for many* — both presuppose that patterns exist universally. Neither establishes universality as the default prior. They tell you what to do once you're in pattern-seeking mode; this principle tells you to be in that mode by default.

The behavioural shift: without it, pattern-seeking is something you remember to do. With it, uniqueness is the claim that requires justification — universality needs no defence.

## Why meta to the two existing principles

- *Find the pattern, not the instance* — names the method. Presupposes patterns exist to be found.
- *Build once for many* — names the action. Presupposes the problem is universal.
- *What holds for one holds for many* — names the prior that makes both rational.

Remove it and both lose their foundation. State it and both become derivable from it.
