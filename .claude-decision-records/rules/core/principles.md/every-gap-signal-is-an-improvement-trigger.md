# Decision Record — Every gap signal is an improvement trigger

**Item:** Principle — Culture / Evolving by Instinct  
**Date:** 2026-03-31  
**Conviction:** high | high

---

## Trigger

During a Socratic inquiry on designing a corrections retrospective process, a question and a correction were shown to be semantically equivalent — both are gap signals at different certainty levels. The compression exposed a principle the network was missing: two existing rules ("Every question is an opportunity to improve the artefact" and the new "Every correction is an opportunity to improve the process") had no named parent. They existed as isolated instances of a pattern the network did not yet name.

## Decision

Add "Every gap signal is an improvement trigger" as a principle under Culture / Evolving by Instinct.

Reparent "Every question is an opportunity to improve the artefact" (communication.md) from "Every communicative artefact serves the reader's context" to this principle in hierarchy.md. The rule text stays in communication.md — its scope remains artefact-specific. Only the hierarchy parent changes.

Add "Every correction is an opportunity to improve the process" (ai-human-conventions.md) as a new rule under this principle.

Add "Gap signal" to the glossary.

## Why Evolving by Instinct, not Transparent by Nature

The surface temptation is Transparent by Nature — gap signals make gaps visible. But the principle does not stop at visibility. The invariant is that the signal *triggers improvement*. Visibility is a byproduct; the driver is evolution. Evolving by Instinct governs.

## Why "Every question" moves parents

Its previous parent — "Every communicative artefact serves the reader's context" — governs how to write. "Every question" governs what to do after writing, when feedback arrives. The motion is different. Its true parent is the gap-signal principle. The rule text remains in communication.md because it applies specifically to communicative artefacts; only the hierarchy position changes.

## Why "create the home" does not duplicate "A misfit is a signal"

"A misfit is a signal — redesign the container or create one" (Pattern-seeking by Instinct) fires in the design phase: when placing or building, find or create the right structure. "If no home exists, create the home" fires in the response phase: when a gap signal arrives during operation and there is nowhere to route it. Same underlying logic; distinct governed territory.

## Wording decisions

- "Route each signal to where the gap lives" — "each signal" names the subject being routed (not the gap, not the work) to distinguish from "Route gaps to their owner" (Protect assigned scope), which routes *work* to a *Steward*.
- "Create the home" over "create it" — "it" is grammatically ambiguous; "gap" is the nearer noun, producing the wrong reading. "Create the home" is unambiguous.
- The original draft included a closing paragraph explaining strand placement. Removed — explanatory, not constraining. The reasoning lives here.
