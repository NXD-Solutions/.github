# Principles are processes

**Date:** 2026-03-12

**Status:** Accepted

**Conviction (S|L):** high | high

**What triggered it:** An authoring session — the question "what process does 'Goal before solution' name?" surfaced that principles are not abstract values but traceable to processes or required parts of processes. The test distinguished principles from DNA (values without a process form) and from rules (implementations against a specific tool).

**What was decided:**

Principle added to `principles.md` under `# Culture / Transparent by Nature`, as the topmost principle: a principle names a process, or a required part of one — something that must be present whenever relevant decisions are made, work is performed, or outcomes are reviewed. Boundary: if it cannot name its process or place in one, ask whether it is DNA, a rule, or noise.

**Why Transparent by Nature:** A principle without process form is invisible — it cannot be observed, challenged, or held. Transparency requires that principles manifest as something traceable.

**Why the tool test belongs here:** The distinction between a principle and a rule emerged from the process discussion: rules implement a principle against a specific tool. Change the tool, the rule changes. Change the tool, the principle holds. This test belongs in the principle's own record as the clearest expression of the boundary.

**Why "process or part of one":** "Goal before solution" is not a full process — it is a required step within any decision or proposal process. The original draft said "names a process" which failed this test. "Or a required part of one" was added to preserve coverage.
