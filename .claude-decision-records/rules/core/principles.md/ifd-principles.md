## IFD principles

**Date:** 2026-04-09
**Authors:** Peter Sixhøj & Claude Sonnet 4.6
**Concern:** Four principles implicit in the IFD glossary entry had no standing in the rule network — they existed only as compressed procedural steps inside a glossary definition. They could not be referenced by name, enforced independently, or evolved without touching the IFD entry.
**Decision:** Promote four principles to `principles.md` under Pattern-seeking by Instinct: Invariants before options, Test simultaneously not serially, Invariants are not choices, Failed candidates are teachers. These are the evaluation and elimination mechanics of IFD — distinct from the method's framing (IFD in the glossary) and its disposition (*Challenge until conviction* in Evolving by Instinct).

**Alternatives considered:** Leaving them in the IFD glossary entry — rejected because glossary definitions carry meaning, not obligation; principles without standing cannot be referenced or enforced. Sub-ruling "Invariants before options" under *Goal before solution* — rejected because the obligation differs in kind: goal-before-solution is a communication constraint; invariants-before-options is a design quality constraint.
