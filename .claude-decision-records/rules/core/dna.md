## Transparent by Nature — naming

**Date:** 2026-03-07

**Authors:** Peter Sixhøj & claude-sonnet-4-6

**Concern:** The first strand needed a name that reflected whether transparency is intrinsic to NXD's character or something deliberately engineered.

**Decision:** "by Nature" over "by Design". Transparency is not engineered into NXD — it is intrinsic. We are transparent without being asked. This also established that "by Nature" is reserved for genuinely intrinsic strands — not all strands qualify.

**Alternatives considered:** "by Design" — rejected, implies transparency is engineered rather than innate.

**Conviction:** high | high

**Examples:** "When something is opaque, we ask: what would make this visible?" — the question is reflexive, not prompted by a rule.

## Evolving by Instinct

**Date:** 2026-03-08

**Authors:** Peter Sixhøj & claude-sonnet-4-6

**Concern:** Continuous improvement was present implicitly in the system but had no DNA strand. The instinct to improve was unnamed.

**Decision:** "Evolving by Instinct" — the drive to improve is reflexive, not deliberate. Introduces a third suffix alongside "by Nature" and "by Design": "by Instinct" = we act on it without being prompted. Lean tension resolved: Lean operates within a cycle (don't over-engineer now); Evolving operates across cycles (improve tomorrow). Different axes — not overlapping.

**Related:** Lean by Design (resolved tension), Evolving by Instinct (parent)

**Alternatives considered:** "Evolving by Design" — rejected, the drive is instinctive not engineered. "Evolving by Nature" — rejected, "by Nature" is reserved for intrinsic qualities like transparency. "Growing by Design" — rejected, "growing" reads as financial.

**Conviction:** high | high

**Tests:** Is it DNA? Yes — drives decisions across Engineering, Culture, Architecture. Is it distinct? Yes — Lean removes, Evolving improves across cycles. Is it meta? Yes — no technology names.

## Evolving by Instinct — bridge sentence to Lean

**Date:** 2026-03-09
**Authors:** Peter Sixhøj & claude-sonnet-4-6

**Concern:** The strand wording "What is lean today can be improved tomorrow" described the relationship between Evolving and Lean but was ambiguous — "improved" implied the lean state was deficient, and the sentence could be read as Lean being a step in Evolving rather than a parallel identity. An audit flagged it as potentially blurring the distinction between the two strands.

**Decision:** Rephrased to "Today's lean state is tomorrow's starting point." The word "state" is the key — it names Lean as a condition reached (a genuine achievement), not a process. Evolving then acts on that state as a foundation for the next improvement cycle. The relationship is Evolving operates on the Lean state, not through it. The sentence was originally added to make the bridge between the two strands explicit; the rephrasing preserves that intent while making the direction unambiguous.

**Related:** Lean by Design (resolved tension), Evolving by Instinct (parent record above)

**Conviction:** high | high
