# Sovereign by Design — removing the last sentence

**Date:** 2026-03-08

**Authors:** Peter Sixhøj & claude-sonnet-4-6

**Concern:** The original strand ended with "No dependency may grow so deep that removing it requires rewriting what we are." This read as a constraint — a derived principle — rather than identity.

**Decision:** Remove the last sentence. The first two sentences carry the full identity: we own our choices, we preserve our ability to change, we survive even when our partners don't.

**Alternatives considered:** Rephrase as "What we depend on never defines what we are" — rejected, removal is cleaner.
