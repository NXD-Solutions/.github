# Goal before solution

**Date:** 2026-03-11

**Status:** Accepted

**Conviction (S|L):** high | high

**What triggered it:** A session pattern — a solution was proposed (per-workflow token reporting), the goal was named (cost management), and scope expanded to the right level (org-wide spend visibility). The pattern produced both the principle and two entries in dna-workbench patterns learned.

**What was decided:**

Principle added to `principles.md` under `# Culture`, tagged *Transparent by Nature*: name the goal before proposing a solution. Without a stated goal, intent is invisible — solutions cannot be evaluated, challenged, or steered. Boundary clause: self-evident actions are exempt; the trigger is consequence or alternatives.

**Why `principles.md`, not `ai-human-conventions.md`:** The obligation applies to any proposal — architectural, engineering, cultural — not only to AI-human exchanges. Placing it in `ai-human-conventions.md` would scope it too narrowly and miss its application in code design, specs, and decisions.

**Why the boundary clause:** Without it, the principle reads as requiring a goal statement for every action — including trivial ones. The clause names the trigger explicitly: consequence or the presence of alternatives.

**Why the watchtower metaphor was excluded:** The metaphor ("the watchtower that makes navigation possible in the dark") is vivid and earned its place in the session, but it is poetic rather than instructive. Rules are spare; rationale belongs in decision records.

**Two lessons in patterns learned:** Lesson 1 (surface the goal to enable evaluation) and lesson 2 (a stated goal opens the solution space upward) were captured as separate entries. They are distinct: lesson 1 is about evaluation; lesson 2 is about generalisation. Collapsing them loses the second insight.
