# Communicative Artefact

Pre-writing gates for communicative artefacts. Parent principle: *Every communicative artefact serves the reader's context — not the system's structure*.

## Define Audience before writing

Who is the specific reader? Name them before the first word is written.

## Define Topic before writing

What domain does the artefact stay within? Any content outside that domain is excluded regardless of relevance to the producing system.

## Define Trigger before writing

What situation brought this reader here right now? The trigger determines what context they carry and what they need to leave with.

## Reader-need filter

Before publishing any paragraph: does this paragraph exist because the reader needs it, or because the system produces it? If the latter — exclude it.

## Every element earns its place

Does this element belong, and is the reader ready for it? An element in the right position that doesn't belong is noise. An element that belongs but arrives before the reader can receive it is a sequencing failure.

## Facets

Quality patterns that enhance a communicative artefact. Applied during writing. The list grows as new patterns are identified.

- **Action examples** — when the artefact expects the reader to act, include an example. The example lets the reader test their understanding of the preceding text.

## Inlining authority-owned facts

A communicative artefact may inline a fact owned by another authority when the reader would otherwise need to leave the document to understand the current paragraph. The inline copy must be recognisably a convenience, not a replacement — reference the authority alongside it. Accept the drift risk only when the reader value is clear; when it is not, reference alone.
