# Test — Every gap signal is an improvement trigger

Principle under Culture / Evolving by Instinct.

---

## Observable behaviours this principle requires

### B1 — Gap signals are acted on, not discarded

When a question, correction, or observation arrives that identifies something not working as expected, the response is to act on it — not to dismiss it or mark it resolved without change. A response that closes the signal without routing it to its source fails this principle.

**Check:** Given a correction about a rule, does the workbench route it to the rule (or the process that produced it)? Given a question about an artefact, does the author surface the relevant text or flag the gap?

### B2 — Routing goes to where the gap lives, not to a Steward

The signal is routed to the artefact, process, or mechanism where the gap exists — not generically to "whoever owns it." Routing to a Steward is the action of "Route gaps to their owner" (Protect assigned scope). Routing to the *location* of the gap is this principle.

**Check:** If a question reveals a gap in communication.md, the response targets communication.md — not "the author of communication.md."

### B3 — Absent homes are created, not tolerated

If a gap signal arrives and there is no artefact or mechanism to receive it, the absence is treated as a gap itself. A new home is created rather than the signal being absorbed without record.

**Check:** A correction about a recurring pattern with no existing record → a record is proposed or created, not logged mentally and forgotten.

### B4 — "Every question" rule is a concrete instance of this principle

The rule "Every question is an opportunity to improve the artefact" is governed by this principle, not by "Every communicative artefact serves the reader's context." Its scope remains artefact-specific; only the hierarchy parent differs.

**Check:** A question about a communicative artefact triggers artefact improvement behaviour — consistent with this principle and its narrower instance.

### B5 — "Every correction" rule is a concrete instance of this principle

The rule "Every correction is an opportunity to improve the process" is governed by this principle. Corrections are captured; patterns across corrections drive process improvement.

**Check:** A correction is made → it is captured (e.g. corrections log) → patterns across corrections are assessed for process-level improvement, not one-off fixes.

---

## Non-behaviours (what this principle does not require)

- Does not require every gap to be fixed immediately — routing is the requirement, not instant resolution.
- Does not require a Steward to own every gap before acting — "create the home" is the instruction when no home exists.
- Does not govern outbound signals (consequences of change) — that is "Communicate consequences of change."
